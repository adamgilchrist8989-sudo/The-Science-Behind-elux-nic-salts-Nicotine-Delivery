<h2><strong>What Are Nic Salts, Really?</strong></h2>
<h3><strong>The Chemistry Breakdown</strong></h3>
<p>Let's get one thing straight: nic salts aren't actually salt like what you'd sprinkle on chips. The name comes from the chemical structure, not the taste.</p>
<p>In their natural state, alkaloids exist in tobacco leaves as salts. When manufacturers extract and process them for traditional vaping, they convert them to freebase form. Think of it like this: freebase is the stripped-down, raw version. Salts are the gentler, more stable cousin.</p>
<p>The key difference? An acid gets added to the freebase molecule. This creates a chemical bond that changes everything about how it behaves in your body. Common acids used include benzoic acid, citric acid, or levulinic acid.</p>
<h3><strong>Why the Molecular Structure Matters</strong></h3>
<p>Here's where it gets interesting. The salt formation lowers the pH level substantially. Freebase sits around 8-9 on the pH scale, making it harsh and alkaline. Salts drop down to 5-6, closer to neutral territory.</p>
<p>Your body recognises this lower pH as less aggressive. That's why <strong><a href="https://simbavapes.co.uk/collections/elux-nic-salts">Elux Nic Salts</a></strong> feel smoother on the inhale. There's less throat irritation, even at higher concentrations.</p>
<p>The molecular weight changes too. Salt molecules are slightly heavier and more stable. They don't degrade as quickly when heated, which means more consistent delivery with each puff.</p>
